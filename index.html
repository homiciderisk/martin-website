About

My research studies the political causes and consequences of housing affordability crises in some of the world’s most productive cities. I show how rising housing price inequality shapes government support and the electoral fortunes of right-wing populist parties. My current work examines resistance to local housing development, the role of local institutions in land-use policy, and whether opponents of new housing are more likely to sort into high-cost cities.

More broadly, I study representation and accountability in local politics, including how challenger parties gain power, the political influence of homeowners, and how political responsibility translates into electoral accountability.
